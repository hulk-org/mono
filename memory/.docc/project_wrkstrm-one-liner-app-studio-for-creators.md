wrkstrm's canonical one-liner is: **"wrkstrm is an app studio for creators."**

That's the *what*. Substrate-discipline (typed schemas, commissioned-agent organization, refinement primitives, etc.) is the *how/moat* — load-bearing but second in any pitch, not first.

**Why:** On 2026-05-18, while drafting Speedrun-style investor copy, rismay locked in "wrkstrm is an app studio for creators" as the lead line. Earlier drafts had led with substrate-jargon ("typed-substrate studio"), which is the moat phrased as the product. That ordering is backwards for an investor read — partners pattern-match the first phrase. The line maps directly onto Speedrun's stated thesis ("creators, hackers, engineers building consumer products that touch culture").

**How to apply:**
- Default opener for wrkstrm-facing copy is "wrkstrm is an app studio for creators." Earn the substrate framing in the body, never as the lead.
- When the audience is *not* investor-facing (engineering peers, partner teams), substrate-first framing is fine — but for any pitch/marketing/fundraising surface, "app studio for creators" leads.
- Open follow-up: which *kind* of creator is the wedge — "app studio for indie iOS developers"? "for solo creators shipping on iOS"? "for content creators"? Broadest reads generic; sharper wedge reads defensible. rismay hasn't picked the wedge yet.
- Consumer iOS apps is the current portfolio center; web is graduating in (rismay.me + server-side-frontend-swift shootout, May 2026).
