# 2026-05-18 — Investor Copy Craft Rules

Three claude-craft rules that landed during a Speedrun investor-form
session. Apply by default whenever rismay asks for investor /
marketing / pitch / customer-facing copy.

## Rule 1 — Value-driven over technical-feature framing

For external-facing copy, **lead with what users get, not with what we
built.** Substrate vocabulary ("typed substrate," "refinement
primitives," "commissioned-agent organization," "ordinality tables,"
"supersession-not-silent-edit") is engineering-speak that alienates the
value-pattern-matching reader. Engineering substance lives as
*background that justifies the value claim*, not as the foreground
claim itself.

- Strong leads: *"Most creators have ten app ideas and ship one. We
  help them ship ten."* / *"Studio-grade polish, solo-creator
  economics."*
- Weak leads (for external): *"typed-substrate studio,"* *"polyglot
  Swift monorepo,"* *"commissioned-agent organization with identity
  bundles."*
- Per-audience rule: external = plain English; internal / engineering
  peers / substrate doctrine = typed vocabulary stays correct.

## Rule 2 — No substrate-ghost framing in external copy

"AI ecosystem" / agent-soup language reads as the founder *being* a
substrate-ghost or disappearing into an AI ecosystem, rather than as a
named human using AI tools. Investors fund people who *use* AI as
tooling; they get cautious about people who *are* an AI ecosystem.

- For the leverage explanation (how a duo / solo ships at studio
  scale), use concrete tooling language: *"with AI tooling tuned so a
  duo ships at studio scale"* / *"a tightly-tuned engineering pipeline
  that lets a duo carry studio output."*
- Avoid: "AI ecosystem," "commissioned agents," "agent organization,"
  "biographer/code-carrier/orchestrator" by name in external copy,
  "ghost-shell," anything that names individual substrate agents as
  if they're co-workers.
- Founders are named humans. The substrate is *engineering
  infrastructure they built*, not *entities they co-found with*.

## Rule 3 — No "rismay" nickname in authoritative copy

For founder bios, investor forms, board/exec-level surfaces, anywhere
institutional credibility is being scored — use the legal name
**Cristian Monterroza** only. Do not parenthesize "rismay," do not
lead with alter-ego framing (anime-Hilbert-space etc.), do not let the
nickname leak into any sentence the reader weights for authority.

- Opener for wrkstrm investor copy: *"Cristian Monterroza."* No
  parenthetical.
- Alter-ego origin (anime / undeveloped manga world) stays in internal
  substrate work, personal projects, rismay.me — never in investor
  copy.
- "rismay" the name remains correct in: rismay.me biographical
  content, internal substrate doctrine and agent commission contracts,
  personal-register conversations, engineering peer audiences. The
  cleavage is authority-scored vs. not.

## Combined application

These three rules combined define the wrkstrm investor-copy register:
plain-English value claim leads → engineering substance justifies in the
body → named humans, not ecosystems → legal names, not nicknames.

Apply by default when drafting investor / accelerator / pitch /
fundraising copy. Don't wait for the correction.
