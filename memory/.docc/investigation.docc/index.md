Investigation reports from Claude's session work. Each article is one investigation outcome — what was investigated, what was found, what's proposed, what's open. Investigations are pre-decision artifacts: they exist to expose the shape of a problem clearly enough that an operator can choose a direction.

## Conventions

- One article per investigation, dated in the filename (`<topic>-<YYYY>-<MM>-<DD>.md`).
- Lead with a one-paragraph summary so the reader can decide whether to read on.
- Always end with **Open questions** and **Recommended next move** — investigations are useless if they don't propose a direction the operator can accept, redirect, or reject.
