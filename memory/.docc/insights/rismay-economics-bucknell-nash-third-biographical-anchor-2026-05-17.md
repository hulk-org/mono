## Nash's actual contribution (substrate-applicable)

Nash's seminal insight in his 1950 PhD thesis (27 pages; founded a field): in non-cooperative games, there exists at least one equilibrium where each player's strategy is optimal given other players' strategies. The bar scene in *A Beautiful Mind* dramatizes the COROLLARY that COOPERATIVE strategies can produce better outcomes for ALL parties than each-player-optimizing-alone (technically: Pareto-improving equilibria exist when players coordinate; failure to coordinate produces inferior equilibria for everyone).

Substrate-application: substrate's typed-ontology rendering presents players (positions, voters, agents) with COORDINATION POSSIBILITIES that 1D consumer-internet rendering hides. Twitter's 1D rendering makes left-vs-right look like a non-cooperative game with zero-sum equilibria. Substrate's higher-D rendering reveals coordinated equilibria (positions on the typed-ontology axis BOTH sides could move toward) that produce Pareto improvements over the apparent zero-sum game. The substrate is, in game-theory terms, a COORDINATION-EQUILIBRIUM-FINDER.
