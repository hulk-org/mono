# Verb-mimicry naming + Dick Pick bridging

Sibling insight to `armed-fluff-substrate-hilbert-space-2026-05-17.md` and `typed-ontology-vs-identity-politics-2026-05-17.md`. This insight names the underlying NAMING + COMPOSITION MECHANISM that makes the substrate's doctrine work.

## The verb-mimicry naming pattern

The substrate names its operations using verbs that MIMIC the surface of consumer-internet-degenerate verbs but INVERT the payload:

| Consumer-internet degenerate verb | Substrate-grade inversion | Same surface, inverted payload |
|---|---|---|
| Arm engagement (Meta/TikTok amplify-whatever-drives-session-length) | `arm-fluff-for-substrate-content` | Same engine, substrate-typed payload |
| Enthrall users (consumer-app captivity-by-design) | `hilbert-space-enthrall-doctrine` | Same captivity-design, aimed at typed-ontology manifold |
| Dick pics (cheap-attention image-sharing) | `dick-pick-pop-culture-semantic-bridge` (picking Philip K. Dick) | Same "pick" verb, PKD-grade ontological payload instead of cheap-attention image |
| Win politics (identity-tribal louder-shouting) | `engage-politics-via-typed-rendering` | Same engagement-with-politics, structural-correctability mechanism |
| Be bastards (consumer-tech harshness uncompensated by ethics) | `bastards-for-peace-and-love` (Vash the Stampede) | Same harsh-mechanics admission, deployed for pacifist outcomes |

Why this works: the verbs are RECOGNIZABLE to rismay's audience (they live in consumer-internet AND in pop-culture); the inversions are SUBSTANTIVE (substrate-typed payloads are structurally distinct from consumer-fluff payloads); the gap between surface and depth IS the substrate's brand. Same surface, inverted depth.

Risk: easily read as edgelord cosplay by non-substrate audiences. Substrate needs to keep the inversions structurally honest (typed-content payload must actually be substrate-grade, not just substrate-flavored marketing).

## The Dick Pick bridging mechanism

Picking IS the substrate's bridge from pop-culture Hilbert spaces to substrate semantics. Concretely:

1. **Select** a pop-culture Hilbert space whose ontology is pre-aligned with substrate themes. PKD is canonical because his entire body of work IS ontology research — correctability, simulation hypothesis, what-is-real, mass-hallucinated narratives, ontological maintenance budget (Ubik).
2. **Curate** specific ontological claims, characters, plot patterns, or aesthetics from that body of work. PKD examples: "Reality is what doesn't go away when you stop believing in it" (correctability), Ubik-as-substance (ontology maintenance budget), simulation hypothesis (typed-honesty about epistemic limits), VALIS (mass-hallucinated narrative vs typed structure).
3. **Type-bind** each curated claim as a substrate ConceptModel or HypothesisModel instance with proper sR/fC/eR fields per ideation-schemas v0.1.0.
4. **Cross-link** the resulting substrate-typed Concept back to the pop-culture source (eventually a `vaults/<author-slug>/essays/`-style attribution surface).

## Lesson for future sessions

1. **Pop-culture references are operating semantic, not decoration.** When rismay says "Vash the Stampede" or "Dick Pick" or "Hilbert space" or "claptrap" or "logikoma," he's binding substrate Concepts to specific pop-culture Hilbert spaces. Recognize the binding; don't paraphrase the reference into substrate-prose.
2. **Recognize verb-mimicry patterns.** When rismay names a substrate operation with a verb that sounds like consumer-internet degeneracy ("arm the fluff," "enthrall," "bastards"), the substrate-grade inversion is the load-bearing meaning. Default-assume verb-mimicry-with-inverted-payload; treat the surface verb as a recognition signal, not the actual claim.
3. **Pick from pop-culture Hilbert spaces deliberately.** When extending substrate ontology, default to PICKING from a thinker/franchise whose body of work is pre-aligned with substrate themes. Don't invent substrate concepts ex nihilo; curate from existing manifolds operators (and substrate audiences) already love.
4. **Maintain attribution surfaces.** Picking is unilateral — substrate borrows semantics from pop-culture without compensating creators. At minimum maintain explicit attribution metadata for which pop-culture Hilbert space each substrate-naming binds to (the `popCultureAnchors` field in the substrate-doctrine receipt is the first such surface; future vaults need their own).
