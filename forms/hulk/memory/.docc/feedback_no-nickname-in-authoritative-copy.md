For authoritative/investor copy (founder bios, investor forms, pitch decks, board-level surfaces, anywhere institutional credibility is being scored) — use the legal name **Cristian Monterroza** only. Do not parenthesize "rismay," do not lead with alter-ego framing (anime-Hilbert-space etc.), and do not let the nickname leak into any sentence the reader weights for authority.

**Why:** On 2026-05-18, while drafting Speedrun founder bios, rismay corrected: "remove the rismay stuff. not authorative." Previous drafts had used *"Cristian Monterroza (rismay)"* as the standard opener and *"who goes by rismay, a name he invented as a kid for an anime world he never had time to develop"* as a maximum-sick opener. Both read as non-authoritative for an institutional investor audience — the nickname is character-grade, not credential-grade, and the alter-ego origin reads eccentric to partners pattern-matching for execution.

**How to apply:**
- For wrkstrm investor-facing bios, the opener is always *"Cristian Monterroza."* Full stop. No parenthetical.
- The alter-ego origin (anime-Hilbert-space, undeveloped manga world) stays in internal substrate work, personal projects, rismay.me biographical surface — never in investor copy.
- "rismay" the name is fine in:
  - rismay.me biographical content (it's the domain)
  - Internal substrate doctrine and agent commission contracts (rismay IS the operator slug)
  - Personal-register conversations and informal copy
  - Engineering peer audiences who recognize the pattern
- "rismay" the name is NOT fine in:
  - Investor pitch decks
  - VC application forms (Speedrun and similar)
  - Board-facing communications
  - Press releases
  - Anything where institutional authority is being scored
- This is the third in a series of investor-copy framing rules:
  1. value-driven-over-technical-framing — lead with what creators get, not what we built
  2. no-ghost-framing-in-external-copy — no AI ecosystem / agent-soup language; founders are named humans
  3. no-nickname-in-authoritative-copy (this one) — legal name only, no alter-ego framing
- These three rules combined define the wrkstrm investor-copy register.
