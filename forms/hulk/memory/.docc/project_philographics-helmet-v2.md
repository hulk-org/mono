In `helmet-racing-league/v2/` (greenfield as of 2026-05-07), each helmet
contains two visual elements:

- **Orb-like objects** — central artifact rendered as an abstract sphere-ish
  object (specifics TBD — could ride the AvatarKit / AvatarBody_Schemas
  genome pipeline, or be procedural).
- **Philographics** — operator-coined term meaning "big ideas in simple
  shapes." Reduces complex concepts (lineage, era, achievement, relation)
  into elemental geometric shapes. Aesthetic adjacent to Apollo mission
  patches, Olympic pictograms, Dieter Rams reduction — NOT phylogenetic
  trees (don't confuse with the misspelling "phylographic").

**Why:** the helmet is the lens/container metadata (team, era, designer);
the orb is the artifact (the helmet rendered as object); the philographic
is the relational context, abstracted into elemental shapes. Maps onto the
"one truth, many lenses" doctrine — same canonical helmet entry, multiple
lens projections per detail card.

**How to apply:** when working in `helmet-racing-league/v2/` or related
visualizer apps, treat philographic regions as "elemental shape, encode big
idea" — favor reduction over network diagrams, family trees, or other
complexity-preserving visualizations. The whole point is the compression
into the simplest shape that still carries the meaning.

Spelling guard: the term is **philographics** (philo- = love of/affinity
for; -graphics = visual); not phylographics (phylo- = lineage/biological
relation), which I initially guessed wrong.
